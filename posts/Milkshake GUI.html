In this post I walk through the process of designing a user interface for a milkshake vending machine (MSVM) utilizing user personas, interface sketches, and design walkthroughs. By the end of this process a winner will emerge which I will attempt to bring to life using Javafx.

USER PERSONAS

Personas are fictional characters that aggregate patterns and trends in a given user group. They can be a very useful point of focus and, from what I've experienced so far, a great opportunity to integrate user empathy into the design process. The Interaction Design Foundation provides a large wealth of free resources, check out their introduction to personas here!

For this assignment I was provided with 2 user personas and tasked with creating a third. In real life projects user personas should be created as a direct refleciton of research and data, when the interface designer or team creates personas without research they are referred to as "Fictional Personas." Using Fictional Personas as a guide for design and development is bad practice but this is what we will be working with for the sake of this assignment.

Scroll down to see the two provided personas alongside my creation: Jeff. To polish things up a bit I created Jeffs Persona with Adobe XD.

TASK DESCRIPTIONS

With personas generated I can look at specific tasks each user would attempt to carry out within the overarching goal of ordering milkshake(s).

INTERFACE SKETCHES

With personas in hand and realistic tasks established I'm ready to get down to sketching Interface ideas. The object here is to generate many ideas quickly, or as my prof put it "to fail fast and fail early." Physical paper is a great medium for this task as it's quick, easy, and (if you recycle) relatively painless!

Once again the folks at the Interactive Design Foundation have a great article on this topic!

MSVM Sketches

User Walkthroughs

After sketching out several ideas and picking the best ones it's time to test them out with user walkthroughs. Prototyping applicaitons like Adobe XD are very powerful in their capacity to animate and provide a realistic experience to test designs with; this is great but paper modals can work brilliantly too.

Ideally a designer would recruit others to play the roles laid out in the persona and make note of any pain points throughout the interaction. In this instance I had to stand in as the user and see what I could gleam by shifting my perspective.

Final Selection & Design Rational

Options are great, but to move forward it's important to identify a design that best resovles the original design problem and user goals. I also don't want to forget about all of those tasty nuggets of information and revealed pain points from the walkthroughs. Identifying how to remedy these problems and putting those changes into action for the next iteration rounds off the process nicely.

Huzzah for empathy driven design! Until next time.
